👤 Member SOPs

Standard Operating Procedures for all AZO members

1. General Conduct

1.1 Communication Standards

  • Maintain professional communication on Discord and in-game
  • Use push-to-talk (PTT) in voice channels during operations
  • Report clear and concise information to your team leader
  • Avoid unnecessary chatter during combat operations

1.2 Attendance & Sign-ups

  • Sign up for operations at least 24 hours in advance when possible
  • Notify your team leader if you need to cancel
  • Arrive 15 minutes before operation start time
  • Be prepared with required mods and loadouts
📌 Note: Consistent attendance is tracked and impacts role assignments for future operations.

2. Pre-Operation Procedures

2.1 Technical Preparation

  1. Verify all required mods are installed and up to date
  2. Test TFAR/ACRE radio functionality
  3. Confirm your microphone and audio settings
  4. Join the appropriate voice channel 15 minutes early

2.2 Briefing Protocol

  • Listen attentively during Zeus briefings
  • Take notes on objectives, ROE, and frequencies
  • Ask clarifying questions only when prompted
  • Confirm your assigned role and equipment

3. During Operations

3.1 Chain of Command

Follow orders from your immediate superior:

  1. Zeus - Overall mission command
  2. Section Leaders - Team tactical command
  3. Fire Team Leaders - Small unit leadership

3.2 Combat Discipline

  • Maintain formation and spacing as directed
  • Follow Rules of Engagement (ROE) strictly
  • Report enemy contact using proper format: "Contact, direction, distance"
  • Conserve ammunition and manage your loadout
  • Provide covering fire when teammates are moving or reloading
⚠️ Warning: Friendly fire incidents must be reported immediately. Accidental teamkills happen, but hiding them is unacceptable.

3.3 Medical & Casualty Procedures

  • Call for medic if wounded: "Medic, [your name], [location]"
  • Provide self-aid when safe to do so
  • Do not leave wounded teammates behind
  • Respect role limits - let medics handle complex injuries

4. Post-Operation

4.1 After Action Review (AAR)

  • Participate in the post-operation AAR
  • Provide constructive feedback
  • Share lessons learned with your team
  • Report any technical issues or mod problems

4.2 Feedback & Ratings

  • Complete the operation feedback form if requested
  • Rate the operation fairly and honestly
  • Provide specific examples in feedback

5. Equipment & Loadouts

5.1 Standard Equipment

Unless specified otherwise, all members should carry:

  • Primary weapon with appropriate ammunition
  • Sidearm (if role permits)
  • Fragmentation grenades (2x minimum)
  • Smoke grenades (various colors)
  • Medical supplies (role dependent)
  • Radio (TFAR/ACRE)
  • Map, compass, watch
📌 Role-Specific Loadouts: Check your team's SOP for specialized equipment requirements (e.g., AT specialists, medics, engineers).

6. Communication Protocols

6.1 Radio Discipline

  • Keep transmissions brief and clear
  • Use proper radio procedure: "This is [callsign], [message], over"
  • Wait for acknowledgment before assuming message received
  • Priority to urgent combat communications

6.2 Callsigns

Standard AZO callsigns:

  • Zeus - Mission command
  • Alpha 1 - Alpha team leader
  • Bravo 1 - Bravo team leader
  • Charlie 1 - Charlie team leader
  • Blackjack - Air support

7. Special Situations

7.1 Respawn Procedures

  • Wait for clearance from Zeus before respawning
  • Regroup with your team at designated rally points
  • Inform your team leader when back in action

7.2 Technical Issues

  • Report crashes or disconnects in Discord immediately
  • Attempt to rejoin as quickly as possible
  • If unable to rejoin, notify your team leader
